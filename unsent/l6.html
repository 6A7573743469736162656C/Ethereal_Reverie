Versailles, April 17, 2025

This morning reminded me so much of the summers I used to spend at my grandma’s. The scent of fresh coffee in the air, that gentle morning chill...

My first impression of Versailles struck me just after noon: hardly any black people here. It’s a city I think I could grow to love. Few tourists, quiet streets, stunning architecture… There’s a sense of stillness that speaks to me.

By early afternoon, I realized something unexpected. Springtime Versailles reminds me of the Moscow suburbs in summer. The way the light filters through the trees, the warmth in the air, it brought back memories I hadn’t touched in years.

Now, as I write this, it’s nearly evening. I’m sitting on the steps near a birch tree. I plucked two small leaves from its branches and tucked them into my diary. There’s no one around. The sun is setting. Solitude wraps around me like a soft blanket. It’s everything I love.

And yet… I find myself wishing for you to be here with me, to witness it all. To share this moment with me. To feel the quiet, to breathe in this peace. To just be.

— V.
